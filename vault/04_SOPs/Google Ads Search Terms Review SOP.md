# SOP: Google Ads Search Terms Review

## When to Use
Weekly during optimization, and within the first 72 hours of any new Search/PMax launch. The single highest-leverage routine for cutting wasted spend.

## Step 1: Pull the Report
- Campaigns → Search keywords → Search terms (or Insights → Search terms for PMax).
- Date range: last 7-14 days (or since launch for new campaigns).
- Sort by Cost descending. Add columns: Cost, Conversions, Conv. rate, CPA.

## Step 2: Classify Each Term
Work top of spend down. Bucket every meaningful term:
- **Convert / keep** — converting at/under target. Leave it; if it's a high-volume converter, add as its own keyword.
- **Add as Exact/Phrase** — relevant, converting or clearly high-intent → promote to keyword (Exact for proven, Phrase to capture variants).
- **Add as Negative** — irrelevant, no intent, or spending with no conversions → negative.

## Step 3: Spot the Junk to Negate
- [ ] Irrelevant terms (wrong service, wrong product)
- [ ] Job-seeker terms (`jobs`, `careers`, `hiring`, `salary`, `employment`)
- [ ] Research/free intent (`free`, `DIY`, `how to`, `template`, `reviews`)
- [ ] Competitor brand names (unless intentionally bidding on them)
- [ ] Wrong geo / out-of-service-area terms

## Step 4: N-Gram / Wasted-Spend Lens
- Scan for recurring words/phrases across many terms that all underperform (e.g. every term with "cheap" or "used").
- Negate the n-gram once at list level instead of one-off terms — kills the whole pattern.
- Prioritize by total cost wasted, not term count.

## Step 5: Negative List Management
- Single-campaign junk → campaign-level negatives.
- Universal junk (jobs, free, competitor names) → shared negative list applied across all of the client's campaigns.
- Maintain one master "global junk" shared list per client; reuse common terms across clients where applicable.
- Watch match-type: a phrase negative blocks more than exact — don't accidentally block converting traffic.

## Step 6: Cadence
- New campaigns: check at 24h, 72h, then weekly.
- Established campaigns: weekly as part of Weekly Optimization SOP.

## Step 7: Log
- Note negatives added, terms promoted to keywords, and estimated wasted spend cut in the client Reporting Log.
- Add any recurring patterns/learnings to client's Agent Memory.

## Notes
- Never negate a term that has converted, even once, without checking total value.
- Don't over-negate on new accounts — let the algorithm gather signal; cut only clear waste early.
