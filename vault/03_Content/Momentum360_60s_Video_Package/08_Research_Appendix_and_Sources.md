## Narrative grounding (for the producer)
- **Discovery is shifting from "ten blue links" to one AI answer.** Zero‑click behavior rose sharply after AI Overviews launched; when an AI answer shows, users click out far less and click a *cited* source rarely — so **being the cited source matters more than ranking #4**. (Pew, 2025.)
- **AI cites what it can parse:** clean direct answers, **structured data/schema** (`LocalBusiness`, `FAQPage`, `Product`, `Organization`), entity authority/E‑E‑A‑T, a **complete, fresh Google Business Profile**, and **rich machine‑readable visual content** (Google now interprets images/immersive media). This is exactly the Scene 2 → Scene 4 argument.
- **Immersive visuals do double duty:** they lift human engagement (time‑on‑page, inquiries, bookings) *and* feed AI the rich signals it uses to understand and recommend a business. The 67%‑want‑/94%‑lack gap is the opportunity Momentum 360 fills.

## Accuracy guardrails (do not violate)
1. **Lead with Pew + NAR on screen** — most defensible.
2. **Attribute Matterport / GBP / vendor numbers by name** ("Matterport reports…"). Never present vendor blog figures as neutral fact.
3. **Don't conflate metrics:** "% of searches that are AI‑driven" (~12–18%, vendor est.) ≠ "AI Overviews on ~50% of Google searches" (different measure). Pick one, label it precisely.
4. If a number can't be confirmed at publish, use the **citation‑free claims** in `04`'s stat‑swap net.
