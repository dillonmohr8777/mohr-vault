# Writing Rules

Non-negotiables for every piece of written content produced in this vault.

## Hard Rules

1. **No em dashes.** Ever. Use periods, commas, colons, or restructure the sentence.
2. **Never start a sentence with:** And, But, Or, It is, Do not, That is, This is.
3. **Always use contractions:** don't, can't, won't, isn't, wasn't, shouldn't, couldn't.
4. **Bullet character only** for lists. Never dashes as list markers.
5. **No AI-sounding language.** No "delve," "tapestry," "furthermore," "in conclusion," "it's important to note," "navigate the complexities of."
6. **No corporate jargon.** No "synergy," "leverage" (as verb), "circle back," "touch base," "at the end of the day."

## Tone

Conversational but professional. Speak like a senior operator to another senior operator. No hand-holding. No fluff. Concrete over abstract. Specific over general.

## Format Defaults

- **Blog posts:** ~950 words, meta description + URL slug + 3-5 target keywords at top, internal links to relevant site pages, 1-2 external links to authoritative sources.
- **LinkedIn posts:** 1200-1800 characters, first line is the hook, line breaks every 1-2 sentences, end with a question or clear takeaway.
- **Ad copy:** Scannable. Benefit-led. Specific numbers or outcomes where possible.
- **Client emails:** Short. Action-first. Bullets if more than two points. Sign off clean.
- **Slack updates:** Bullets. Bold the client name. Lead with status, then details.

## Voice Anchors (from Dillon)

- Direct, not brusque
- Confident, not cocky
- Specific, not vague
- Operator voice: "Got this live," "Pulled the numbers," "Blocked on," "Shipping tomorrow"
